What is a CIBIL Score & Why it Matters?

A CIBIL Score is a three-digit summary of your credit history, ranging from 300 to 900. It is compiled by credit bureaus using your past repayment patterns, outstanding debts, and card utilization rates.

Lenders use this score as a primary checkpoint to assess your repayment risk. An excellent CIBIL score (above 750) unlocks lower interest rates, faster document processing, and waiver of auxiliary charges.

Tips to Improve Your Credit Score:

  • Pay on time: Avoid delayed credit card bills or active loan installments.
  • Keep utilization low: Limit active credit card utilization to under 30% of limits.
  • Monitor reports: Audit credit summaries annually to fix recording anomalies.
  • Mix credit products: Maintain a healthy balance of secured and unsecured credit lines.
