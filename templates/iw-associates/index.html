Ask Another Question

GST registration is mandatory for service providers with annual turnover exceeding ₹20 Lakhs, and goods suppliers exceeding ₹40 Lakhs (thresholds vary by special states). Necessary documents include PAN card, Aadhaar card, business incorporation proof, address proof of the premises, and bank account details.

Corporate businesses must file annual Income Tax Returns. Additionally, corporations must pay advance taxes quarterly (June 15, Sept 15, Dec 15, March 15) if their estimated annual tax liability exceeds ₹10,000. Under GST, filing frequencies (monthly or quarterly) depend on your registration scheme and annual turnover.

Statutory audits are mandatory for all registered Private Limited and Public Limited companies in India under the Companies Act, 2013, regardless of their turnover, capital size, or profit/loss status. The audit must be conducted exclusively by a practicing Chartered Accountant.

MSME compliance begins with registering on the Udyam portal to obtain MSME certifications. It unlocks critical government benefits, including priority sector lending rates, subsidies on intellectual patents, protection against delayed payments (mandatory interest for payments delayed beyond 45 days), and eligibility for corporate tenders.
